How we protect your data.

The privacy and anonymity concept behind the Eunavia self-help and companion apps — explained plainly. Maximum protection for the most sensitive data there is, without losing function or ease of use.

As of June 2026 · Self-help and companion apps in the lifestyle and wellbeing space — not a medical device, not medical or legally binding advice.

1 · What this is about

Our apps accompany people through deeply personal moments — quitting smoking, letting go of alcohol, questions about their own health and wellbeing. This is the most sensitive data there is. That is why privacy at Eunavia is not an afterthought but the foundation everything rests on.

This document summarises how we deliver that protection technically — in plain language, so you can understand why you can trust us. The core idea is separation: who you are and what you record about your health are never kept together.

The central tension — and how we resolve it. “Staying anonymous” and “being able to identify yourself” (e.g. to restore a personal history) only seem to contradict each other. The solution is separation: who a person is is kept apart from what they record about their health. The two halves are linked only by an anonymous tag that reveals nothing on its own.

3 · Anonymity and identification — the split-identity model

People should stay anonymous yet still be able to recognise themselves when needed — and everything should stay simple. This works through two strictly separated vaults, neither of which knows the contents of the other.

Identity vault — “who you are”

Knows, if anything, only an anonymous way in (e.g. your device’s fingerprint or face unlock). No health data. No name, no email needed.

Health vault — “what you record”

Stores your entries, progress and history — but without knowing who you are. No name, no email, no device. Just the content, detached from the person.

anonymous tag  — links the two, reveals nothing alone

Only both halves together would form a complete picture — and that is exactly what we prevent by keeping them apart. Anyone who got hold of one half would hold only a meaningless half.

Recovery without email

A lost device must mean neither data loss nor a mandatory email address. On setup, the app creates a personal recovery key (e.g. a memorable phrase). It lets you restore an optional, end-to-end encrypted cloud history on a new device — entirely without an email address, and without any server ever seeing the plaintext.

4 · One fortress per app

Each product gets its own sealed-off data world with its own keys. This is security through compartmentalisation — and at the same time a privacy gain, because a cross-app profile cannot even come into being.

LayerMeasureEffect
HostingOwn project/account per app, in the EU regionAn incident in one app affects no other.
DatabaseOwn database and credentials per appSeparate stores, no shared keys.
KeysOwn master key per app, own data key per personA single key never unlocks everything.

Deliberately no shared account across apps. A central “Eunavia account” would be convenient — but exactly wrong for health data: it would create a single pot whose leak exposes everything. Instead, every app stays its own fortress.

5 · Encryption & “really deleting”

Protection at every stage — and deletion that works even in backups.

In the cloud (optional)End-to-end: the server only sees unreadable ciphertextServer incidents, curious operators

Deleting that really deletes

The “right to erasure” is often poorly implemented because data survives in backups. With us: instead of laboriously hunting down records, we destroy the personal key. Without it, that person’s entire data — in every backup too — becomes irretrievably unreadable. One tap, effective immediately.

6 · Emergencies without losing function

Privacy must not slow the function down — and there has to be a fast emergency feature. The two are no contradiction: a local emergency path is both faster and more data-minimal.

  • Instant help in critical moments runs completely offline and without an account.
  • Important crisis resources (e.g. emergency and helpline numbers) are built into the app — no online lookup needed.
  • An optional trusted contact is stored only locally; triggering it calls directly, without going via a server.
  • At warning signs, the app points kindly but firmly to human and medical help — rather than “treating” you itself.

The key point. Because the most effective functions run on the device anyway, the privacy-friendly architecture is also the fastest and most robust. Your benefit and privacy point in the same direction.

7 · What we never do

  • Sell your data or pass it to third parties.
  • Build advertising or tracking services into our apps.
  • Create a shared profile across all apps.
  • Force you to provide an email address or a login.
  • Make life-saving help depend on an internet connection.

8 · Verifiable — voluntary auditing

We don’t just want to say your data is safe — we lay our protection architecture open and rely on voluntary, independent checks. Our non-medical self-help approach does not require the mandatory certifications of a medical device.
